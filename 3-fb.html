FDIC Resolutions & Key Projects

Critical Banking Transitions

Overview

The FDIC (Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation) plays a critical role in safeguarding the financial system by protecting depositors and ensuring the orderly resolution of failed financial institutions. As a contractor in the Resolutions & Receiverships Division, I managed mission-critical Failed Bank website, delivering accurate, real-time information to the public during closures. My responsibilities spanned the entire Failed Bank lifecycle—from initial closing through post-closing processes and ultimately receivership termination.

Key Contributions
  • Bank Closings Support: Participated in over 250 bank closings across 13 years, including periods of recession when multiple institutions closed on the same day. Coordinated with cross-functional teams to ensure timely, accurate updates to the Failed Bank web pages.
  • Web Content Management: Oversaw the failed bank page template, integrated multi-source updates under tight deadlines; coordinated client reviews to ensure accuracy and compliance before publishing.
  • Traffic Reporting & Analysis: Generated post-closing traffic reports and user behavior analysis for Failed Bank web pages, providing insights into activity, navigation patterns, and engagement trends to inform content and UX decisions.
  • Stakeholder Communication: Acted as the closing point of contact (POC); sent time-sensitive broker-depositor notifications via GovDelivery.
  • High-Impact Delivery: Supported FDIC’s mission to maintain public trust by enabling accurate, real-time dissemination of closure information to depositors, institutions, and the public.
  • Outcome: Enabled transparent, timely public updates that supported depositor confidence and system stability.
Challenges & Impact
  • Multiple Closures at Once: Navigated the complexity of handling simultaneous bank closings during the financial crisis, ensuring updates were processed without delays.
  • Time-Critical Closings: Worked late based on the closing bank’s local time zone to ensure pages were updated immediately after closure announcements and to give support for the closing team.
  • High-Stakes Accuracy: Managed sensitive financial data where errors could impact depositor confidence and public trust.
  • Public Trust & Stability: By delivering accurate updates and clear communication, I directly contributed to FDIC’s mission of maintaining financial stability and depositor confidence during turbulent economic times.

Other Key Projects

Created wireframes, developed a phased migration strategy, established DAM hierarchy, and ensured accessibility compliance. Trained content authors and provided documentation. This project strengthened my expertise in AEM authoring, content governance, and cross-team collaboration.

Receivership Accounting Manual (RAM) Creation (2015)

Solely responsible for designing and launching a digital manual containing 350+ accounting documents across 18 sections. Developed the structure, ensured 508 compliance, and built the manual in AEM with interactive navigation. Collaborated across four accounting branches, managed document versions, and completed the project in three months ahead of an Accounting Conference. Received an appreciation letter from the Division Chief for delivering a high-quality, accessible product on time.

Part of the Divisional Website Transfer to a New Division (2019–2020)

Oversaw the transfer of a 75-page sub-divisional website with 300+ documents to a newly formed division. Coordinated with both divisions, IT specialists, and stakeholders to define requirements and migration timelines. Ensured a smooth transition by preparing documentation, training new division POCs, and enforcing a content freeze to minimize disruption. Managed post-transfer support and even resolved an inquiry a year later, showcasing my strategic planning, attention to detail, and strong communication skills.
